Source Documentation

30 Years of
Industry Evidence.

Every claim in the Green Analytics methodology traces to a published standard, peer-reviewed study, or industry body report. This page is the documentation trail.

Why this page exists: The events industry has produced 30+ years of sustainability guidance that is rarely cited together and even more rarely applied consistently. GA methodology is not proprietary science — it is the industry's own frameworks, applied with explicit boundaries and traceable attribution. This reference list is the foundation of that claim.
Governing Standards & Frameworks
The three primary frameworks that define GA's measurement architecture
Net Zero Carbon Events (NZCE) Roadmap
UNFCCC / Events Industry Coalition · 2022
Defines the five stakeholder tiers, 90% reduction target, and offset bridge methodology that underpins GA's three-tier architecture.
netzerocarbonevents.org
NZCE Measurement Methodology
Net Zero Carbon Events · 2023
The operational companion to the Roadmap — defines calculation approach, segment boundaries, and the documentation-first evidence standard.
netzerocarbonevents.org
EIC Sustainable Event Standards
Events Industry Council · 2023
Defines the contracted footprint boundary used in GA's Tier 1 space assessments. GA replaces ISO 20121's self-scoped model with EIC criteria.
insights.eventscouncil.org
GHG Protocol — Scope 3 Standard (Category 6: Business Travel)
World Resources Institute / WBCSD · 2011, updated
The basis for GA's Tier 2 attendee carbon footprint attribution — business travel emissions belong to the employer, not the event organizer.
ghgprotocol.org/scope-3-standard
ISO 20121 — Sustainable Events Management System
International Organization for Standardization · 2012, updated 2024
GA methodology is built in the ISO 20121 ecosystem but explicitly replaces the self-determination scope model with EIC criteria to close the measurement gap.
iso.org/iso-20121-sustainable-events.html
Industry Research & Economic Data
Published reports informing GA's industry context and market positioning
EIC Global Significance of Business Events
Events Industry Council / Oxford Economics · 2018
Primary source for industry economic scale data. 1.87 million business events documented in the US in 2016.
insights.eventscouncil.org
Finding the Future Together: Towards a More Sustainable B2B Trade Show Industry
SISO / UFI · September 2022
Documents industry sustainability barriers: financial (18 references), cultural (15), operational (11). Confirms structural absence of measurement infrastructure.
siso.org/finding-future-together
NZCE Reporting Results — Full Report
Net Zero Carbon Events · 2025
Documents the 30+ inconsistent calculators and measurement approaches across the global events industry — the core indictment of the pre-GA measurement landscape.
netzerocarbonevents.org
ESCA Sustainability Guidance (Parts 1 & 2)
Exhibit Services & Contractors Association · 2024
Trade show supplier sustainability framework — informs GA's Tier 3 exhibitor assessment and supplier intelligence program architecture.
esca.org/resources/sustainability
Year of the Sustainable RFP
Smart Meetings · 2023
Documents the shift in corporate buyer expectations — sustainability criteria moving from optional to required in event procurement.
smartmeetings.com
Emission Factors & Calculation Methodology
Science-based sources for GA's emissions conversion factors
DEFRA / UK BEIS Greenhouse Gas Conversion Factors
UK Department for Energy Security & Net Zero · Annual
Primary emission factor source for transportation and energy calculations. Used in GA's Tier 2 attendee carbon footprint scoring.
gov.uk — Conversion Factors for Company Reporting
EPA Emission Factors Hub
US Environmental Protection Agency · Updated annually
US-specific emission factors for stationary combustion, mobile sources, and electricity generation by grid region.
epa.gov/climateleadership/ghg-emission-factors-hub
ICAO Carbon Emissions Calculator
International Civil Aviation Organization
Aviation emission factors used in GA's Tier 2 flight segment calculations, including cabin class radiative forcing adjustments.
icao.int — Carbon Offset Calculator
Reducing Food's Environmental Impacts Through Producers and Consumers
Poore & Nemecek · Science, 2018
Peer-reviewed lifecycle analysis of food emissions by protein and production method. Foundation for GA's Food & Beverage segment scoring.
doi.org/10.1126/science.aaq0216
Cornell Hotel Sustainability Benchmarking Index (CHSB)
Cornell University Center for Hospitality Research · Annual
Industry-standard hotel sustainability benchmarking used in GA's Tier 2 Housing segment assessment. Provides peer-comparable energy and water performance data.
greenview.sg/chsb-index
EPA Energy Star Program — Appliance Energy Consumption
US Environmental Protection Agency
Reference for device wattage standards used in GA's Tier 2 Energy Consumption segment — laptops, phones, tablets, and other personal electronics.
energy.gov — Estimating Appliance Energy Use
Venue Standards & Certification Programs
Building and venue performance frameworks referenced in GA's Tier 1 assessments
LEED v4 / v5 — Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design
US Green Building Council · Current: v4.1
Venue certification standard. LEED-certified properties provide verifiable energy and water performance data that feeds directly into Tier 1 space assessments.
usgbc.org/leed/v5
Las Vegas & the Net Zero Carbon Events Roadmap
Green Analytics / NZCE · 2024
Original GA research documenting Las Vegas as a global proof point for NZCE-aligned event infrastructure — permanent venues, renewable energy commitments, circular production ecosystems.
