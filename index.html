The Pressure Is Real

Event planners are being asked to solve a problem they were never resourced to solve. Sustainability requirements are showing up in RFPs, client contracts, corporate compliance reports, and attendee expectations — often all at once, with no additional budget, no extra staff, and no clear framework to follow.

The old answer was: hire a sustainability consultant. Spend months on a methodology. Collect data from dozens of vendors. Produce a report that might not hold up to scrutiny. And pay for all of it yourself. That model never worked at scale, and it never will.

📋
Corporate clients require it in RFPs
Sustainability sections are now standard in corporate event RFPs. Questions about carbon measurement, vendor sourcing, and ESG alignment appear in procurement documents from Fortune 500 companies — and a non-answer disqualifies the bid.

Compliance deadlines are arriving
The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive is already in effect. California's SB 253 and SB 261 require climate disclosure for companies doing business in the state. The 2030 Paris Agreement targets are four years away. Corporate clients are looking to their events for documented data.

Built on Established Global Standards

We didn't invent anything.
We connected what already exists.

Every claim in the GA model traces to one of three established global frameworks — the same ones being used by global leaders right now. We built the operational system that connects them for how events are actually produced.

🏛️
EIC — Event Industry Council
Industry authority since 1949. The EIC business event definition sets the assessment boundary. GA's process is designed to achieve EIC Bronze certification through standard documentation — no operational changes required.
🌍
NZCE — Net Zero Carbon Events
Launched at COP26. UNFCCC-supported. 600+ organizations across 60 countries agreed on five priority action areas for events. GA's three-pillar assessment is built around those five areas — mapped to the EPA's identified emission sources.
🏗️
LEED — The World's Largest Green Building Program
30+ years of measuring inputs not outputs. Tracking materials from purchasing decisions. Separating owner scope from tenant scope. Preferring existing venues over temporary sites. All of it maps to how professional events are already produced.
