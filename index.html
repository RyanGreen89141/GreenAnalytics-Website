The Pressure Is Real

Event planners are being asked to solve a problem they were never resourced to solve. Sustainability requirements are showing up in RFPs, client contracts, corporate compliance reports, and attendee expectations — often all at once, with no additional budget, no extra staff, and no clear framework to follow.

The old answer was: hire a sustainability consultant or take on the workload yourself. Spend months building a custom program. Collect key pieces of environmental data from the venues. Produce a report 6-8 weeks after the event ends. And pay for all of it yourself. That model never worked at scale, and it never will.

📋
Corporate clients require it in RFPs
Sustainability sections are now standard in corporate event RFPs. Questions about carbon measurement, vendor sourcing, and ESG alignment appear in procurement documents from Fortune 500 companies — and a non-answer disqualifies the bid.
↑ Appearing in the majority of corporate RFPs in 2024–2025
⚖️
Compliance deadlines are arriving
The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive is already in effect. California's SB 253 and SB 261 require climate disclosure for companies doing business in the state. The 2030 Paris Agreement targets are four years away. Corporate clients are looking to their events for documented data.
2030 — the global emissions reduction milestone
🙋
Attendees expect action, not talk
Post-pandemic, event professionals increasingly choose events that reflect their values. 73% of global consumers say they would definitely or probably change their consumption habits to reduce environmental impact. That shift is reaching business events — and planners are expected to respond.
